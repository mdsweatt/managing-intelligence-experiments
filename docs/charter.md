## 0. How to read this document

This is a pre-registration, not a results doc. The hypotheses below each carry an explicit **kill-condition** — the observation that would refute them — written *before* any data is collected. Conclusions are deliberately deferred. Deciding what would change our minds in advance is what makes the eventual conclusions trustworthy rather than motivated.

**Product framing this serves:** a *provocation tool*, not a precision instrument. The goal is to make token consumption legible and a little alarming to people who don't yet know the category exists — not to produce finance-grade numbers. This scopes the required rigor: the experiments exist to make our **ranges honest and non-embarrassing**, not to nail an exact cost. Over-investing in precision is the same trap as building the precision tool we already rejected. The deep version of the analysis is the asset that feeds a paid consulting/training layer; the provocation tool ships on partial results.

## 1. Observation

Individuals and enterprises are beginning to consume "intelligence" (LLM tokens) at scale, and will increasingly need to budget and allocate it the way they budget headcount, capex, and opex. Today almost no one does this: in the originating conversations, upper management told the team to "do what you think is best with the credit card," and most knowledge workers do not know what a token is, let alone what they cost. Subscription products deliberately hide token consumption, so even motivated users have no meter at the surface they work on.

## 2. Question (evolving)

> How can individuals and enterprises manage intelligence in an economically viable and efficient way?

This question is expected to evolve as the technology evolves (model prices fall, routing improves, agentic usage grows). The current sharpened sub-question:

> Can the token cost of knowledge work be measured at the task level reliably enough to compose credible budget ranges for roles and teams?

## 3. Hypotheses (with kill-conditions)

| ID | Hypothesis | Kill-condition |
|----|-----------|----------------|
| **H1 — Stability** | A given task, at a fixed load band *and a fixed model+tokenizer version*, has token cost with bounded **run-to-run** variance — stable enough to express as a band. | Coefficient of variation across repeated identical runs persistently exceeds **~15–20%** within a cell, so no useful band exists. *Pre-registered threshold (from prior-art scan, see §9): under ~15–20% → bandable; over → the product pivots from **predicting** cost to **monitoring** the distribution. This is **within-cell run-to-run** variance — between-model variance is a different axis that feeds the over-service story, not H1, and must not be conflated with it. If H1 dies, point-estimate task budgeting dies; distribution-monitoring may still ship.* |
| **H2 — Additivity** | Role-level cost can be reconstructed bottom-up from task costs via a known rollup rule. | Bottom-up prediction cannot match a real role's measured end-to-end usage within the stated band, even after the H3 corrections. |
| **H3 — Two-factor sufficiency** | Cacheability and fan-out explain *most* of the error between a naive task sum and actual role cost. | After correcting for both, a large unexplained residual remains. Pre-registered candidate additional factors, in priority order: **(a) tool/retrieval payload size**; **(b) multi-turn context accumulation** (history resent every turn — flagged in prior art as possibly the single largest hidden cost, and distinct from single-call payload); **(c) orchestration / retry overhead** (coordination and rework that eats projected margin). See §6. |
| **H4 — Provocation works** | A fat-banded estimate, framed as *relative waste*, lands a one-time reframe that changes how a person or org reasons about intelligence spend — enough to pull them toward the paid accountability layer. | People shown the estimate shrug — no decision changes, no new questions asked. *Human-behavior hypothesis; the business rests on it; must not be crowded out by the measurement experiments. Pre-registered priors (prior-art scan, §9): visibility-only (showback) decays ~10–20%/yr and can **backfire** when absolute cost looks small (license-to-waste); durable change needs accountability (chargeback). So the free tool is the **hook**, not the behavior engine — durable value lives in the paid layer. Experiment 3 tests **waste-ratio vs. absolute-cost framing**; absolute-low is predicted to backfire.* |
| **H5 — Determinism via skill** | Adding a **well-defined skill** (a frozen, structured output scaffold) to a task — model, tokenizer, and input fixture held fixed — **reduces output-token run-to-run variance (CoV) and/or mean output tokens** versus the unscaffolded prompt. I.e. Phase-1a's **Lever A** (constrain the output) is *causally* real, not a 1a correlation. | On the high-output-latitude tasks (#6, #9), skill-on does **not** lower output-token CoV by a practically meaningful margin (and does not lower — or actively raises — mean output tokens) relative to the skill-off arm *at the same model*. Then "skills add determinism" dies as a cost lever and the 1a Lever-A reading was correlational coincidence. **Placebo guard:** the low-latitude control (#4, already ~0% CoV) should show ≈no gain; if it "tightens" too, the effect is a confound, not the skill. |
| **H6 — Tier-agnostic quality via skill** | With the skill applied, a cheaper model (**Haiku**) reaches **quality-equivalence** with a more expensive one (**Opus**) on the same task, *and* the skill **narrows the Haiku→Opus quality gap** more than the unscaffolded prompt does. A well-defined skill substitutes for model tier on quality, *within the task's scope*. | Haiku-with-skill **fails** quality-equivalence vs. Opus-with-skill beyond a pre-registered margin on the frozen judge, **or** the skill fails to narrow the skill-off Haiku→Opus gap. Then skills do **not** make tier interchangeable — quality still needs the bigger model, and the over-service "Haiku does it identically" claim holds only where the judge confirms it, task by task. |
| **H7 — Determinism ≠ cost (cap vs mandate)** | A skill lowers output-token *variance* on any high-latitude task (H5's variance half), but its effect on *mean* output tokens — hence cost — has a **sign predictable a priori**: a scaffold that **caps** (constrains output below the model's natural skill-off length) lowers mean output; one that **mandates** (requires more structure/content than the natural output) raises it. Determinism and efficiency are **separable** levers. *(Converts a 1c **post-hoc** finding — the #6/#9 dissociation the H5 prediction did not foresee — into an a-priori test on fresh tasks.)* | Across the pre-classified ladder, the a-priori **cap/mandate** label does **not** predict the sign of Δ(mean output) — capped tasks fail to get cheaper and/or mandated tasks fail to get more expensive (sign prediction at/below chance or systematically inverted). Then cap-vs-mandate is not the governing split and 1c's dissociation was idiosyncratic to #6/#9. |
| **H8 — Scaffold-specific determinism** | H5's output-variance reduction is caused by the **scaffold's structure** (format/schema + scope/length constraints), not by the mere presence of a `system` block. A length-matched **neutral** system block with no structuring content does not tighten `CoV_output` the way the real skill does. | The neutral-system control arm reproduces **≥ ½** of the skill's `CoV_output` tightening — ratio R = (CoV_off − CoV_neutral) / (CoV_off − CoV_skill) ≥ 0.5 — on the high-latitude tasks. Then part of H5's effect is "any system block / priming," not the scaffold, weakening 1c's causal attribution of the variance drop to the skill. |

### Pre-registered predictions

Kill-conditions say what would *refute* each hypothesis; these say what we *expect* if it holds. Recorded before any of our own measurement so the results can surprise us — not for precision (this is still a provocation tool), but as the single best defense against the confirmation bias this project is built around. If you predict 3–5× and measure 1.2×, you learned something; if you only look afterward, you'll rationalize whatever lands. All magnitudes are priors (some inherited from §9 prior art), to be replaced by Experiment 1–2 output. If the data disagrees, the data wins.

- **H1.** Variance tracks the fan-out axis: discrete / low-fan-out cells come in tight (CoV well under the threshold, expected <~10%); exploratory / high-fan-out cells come in noisy and are where H1 is most at risk of breaching ~15–20%. *Surprised if* a discrete cell is noisy or a high-fan-out cell is tight — either would mean fan-out isn't the variance driver we think.
- **H2.** Direction of the naive-sum error is role-dependent: cache-heavy roles over-count, fan-out-heavy roles under-count; the §4 rollup corrects both to within band. Rough rollup priors: cache deflates **~3–5×**, fan-out inflates **~5–50×** (wide because high-variance by nature).
- **H3.** Cache + fan-out explain *most* of the residual, but a non-trivial remainder persists and loads primarily on **(b) multi-turn accumulation** (prior art's pick for largest hidden cost), secondarily **(a) payload**, least **(c) orchestration**. *Surprised if* the residual loads heavily on a factor we didn't pre-register, or vanishes entirely (two factors were sufficient after all).
- **H4.** Waste-ratio framing changes a decision or prompts a new question for a majority of viewers; absolute-low-cost framing backfires (license-to-waste) for a meaningful fraction. Direction inherited from prior art (§9), not yet our own observation.
- **H5.** On high-latitude generation (#6, #9), skill-on pulls output CoV down toward the bandable floor (<~10%) and holds or *reduces* mean output tokens (explicit scope + length caps cut runaway generation). On the #4 placebo there is ≈nothing to gain (already deterministic). *Surprised if* a high-latitude task fails to tighten, or the placebo tightens further (a confound).
- **H6.** Skill-on shrinks the Haiku→Opus quality gap toward parity on **format/scope-bound** tasks (#6, plausibly #9); where quality rests on latent capability the scaffold cannot supply, the gap persists despite the skill. *Surprised if* the skill closes the gap on a capability-bound task, or fails to close it on a purely format-bound one — either would mean a "well-defined skill" isn't doing the work we attribute to it.
- **H7.** Capped tasks (e.g. #6 short copy, #7 summarize, #8 extract) → mean output **down**; mandated tasks (e.g. #9 long draft, #13 research) → mean output **up**; `CoV_output` **down on both**, subject to a **capability floor** — a model too weak to hold the scaffold (cf. 1c's #9-Haiku, which neither tightened nor shortened) may not move at all. *Surprised if* a capped task gets more expensive or a mandated task cheaper — either inverts the rule. The per-task cap/mandate label = sign(scaffold length constraint − skill-off mean output), frozen in `runs/phase1d.yaml` **before** the N=20 run (no relabeling after data).
- **H8.** The neutral-system arm sits much closer to skill-off than skill-on on `CoV_output` — most tightening needs the actual structure. **Supported** if the neutral arm reproduces **< ⅓** of the skill's reduction (R < 0.33); **ambiguous** in the ⅓–½ band; **killed** at R ≥ ½. Scored only on cells where the skill itself produced a clear reduction (R is unstable near the CoV floor). *Surprised if* a structureless block tightens variance as much as the scaffold — that would make 1a's "Lever A" partly a priming artifact, not the skill.

## 4. Key constructs

**Task at a load.** A bare task name under-specifies cost; the dominant driver is usually input context size. The atomic unit is therefore *task × load band* (small / medium / large), benchmarked at each band rather than as a single number. Output size and iteration depth are secondary knobs folded into the per-task measurement.

**Four-variable decomposition.** Every estimate separates into:

1. **Tokens per task** — measured; relatively stable *within a fixed model+tokenizer version*; the credible floor.
2. **Frequency** — supplied by the user; the one number they actually know; the interactive knob in the product.
3. **Price per token** — volatile (models trend ~10× cheaper over 6–12 months); isolated as a single updatable scalar.
4. **Uncertainty band** — for task-mix drift and model variance.

Core principle: **denominate in tokens, not dollars.** Token prices rot in a quarter; tokens-per-task is far more stable and demand only grows (Jevons). Dollars = token count × an updatable price scalar. This quarantines the volatility into one knob and lets the benchmarked core survive the cost collapse.

> **Caveat (prior-art scan).** Tokens are stable only *within a fixed model+tokenizer*. A tokenizer change moves the token count itself at constant behavior and constant price — observed: Opus 4.7 emitted up to ~35% more tokens than 4.6 for the same input, at unchanged per-token price. This is a hit the dollar-quarantine does *not* catch: it's not price drift, it's the unit moving under us. Consequence: every benchmarked figure is version-stamped by model+tokenizer, and the library is a continuously re-run meter, not a static report.

**Consumption archetype.** A class defined by the *shape* of how a role's token cost scales — not by job function. Defined provisionally by two axes that break the additive rollup:

- **Cacheability** — does the role reload the same large context repeatedly (sublinear; cheap cache reads) or hit fresh context each time (linear)?
- **Fan-out** — single calls, or agentic work that spawns subagents and tool chains (multiplicative, high-variance)?

Provisional 2×2 and its rollup rule:

| Archetype | Cache | Fan-out | Naive sum is… | Rollup rule | Example |
|-----------|-------|---------|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Repetitive | High | Low | over-counts | apply cache discount | support against a fixed KB; coding in one repo |
| Discrete | Low | Low | ~correct | additive baseline | ad-hoc drafting, classification, lookups |
| Exploratory | Low | High | under-counts | fan-out multiplier + fat band | "research this market"; codebase audit |
| Compound | High | High | both effects | combined; widest band | multi-file refactor over a cached repo |

> Caveat: this 2×2 is constructed scaffolding, not an empirical taxonomy. The *real* archetypes should fall out of the benchmark data (cluster roles by measured cost-shape), not be imposed top-down. The archetype is not defined separately from tasks — it **emerges** from the cacheability/fan-out tags on a role's task mix.

## 5. Methods — experiments

### Prerequisites & experimental controls (apply to all experiments)

What must be true before pressing "go" on Experiment 1, and the discipline that makes the run measure what it claims rather than measuring its own setup.

**Controls — fixed vs. varied.** Experiment 1 measures one thing: intrinsic run-to-run spread. So *within a cell*, every controllable input is held fixed — frozen input artifact, exact prompt, model+tokenizer version, thinking/effort setting, temperature. The only thing allowed to move is the model's own stochasticity. *Across cells*, exactly one axis changes at a time (load band, **or** model, **or** thinking on/off), so any cost difference is attributable to that axis; varying two at once destroys attribution.

- **Temperature** is a deliberate choice, not a default: temp 0 suppresses sampling randomness; realistic-default measures the variance users actually live with. Since H1 is about real-world bandability, run **realistic-default, fixed and recorded** — not 0. *Build-time check (do not assume): confirm whether the API permits setting temperature while extended thinking is enabled.*

**No output caps (ruled 2026-06-14).** No `max_tokens` cap. Cost is controlled by **scoping the fixture**, not truncating output: choose inputs and task definitions whose natural completion is bounded. This is load-bearing for the fan-out tasks (#13, #14, #20, #21) — defined over a **frozen, finite input set** ("research across these N fixed pages," not open-web "research this topic"), which bounds cost *and* delivers reproducibility from the same frozen-input principle. Capping mid-generation would corrupt the measurement (a truncated artifact is not a natural one); scoping the task does not.

**Measurement target.** Define what "cost" *is* before computing variance on it. Each run returns a vector — input, output, cache-read, cache-write, tool-result tokens — and the components are not fungible (a cache-read token carries roughly a tenth the dollar weight of a fresh-input token). Record the full vector; compute variance **per component and on a dollar-weighted composite** (price scalars applied at analysis time, kept separate per §4). Headline H1 figure = the composite's spread; the components localize *where* the variance lives. For the over-service claim specifically, each task also needs a pre-defined **output-equivalence criterion** — cost is measured exactly, but "Haiku does it identically" is a quality judgment that must be operationalized, or the claim weakens to "cheaper," not "identical."

**Instrument (harness) requirements.** Beyond the run/capture spec below: stamp model+tokenizer version, run date, and a **hash of the exact fixture + config** on every record so any run replays. Two guardrails tied to cost control: a **pre-run cost estimate with a hard call/spend ceiling** (a loop bug must not be able to empty the card), and **rate-limit handling** (backoff/batching) so a large-input cell can't hit a TPM wall mid-run.

**Materials (fixtures).** Frozen artifacts + exact prompts, one per (task × load band) — the only prerequisite that is genuine curation labor; the rest is decisions plus a few hundred lines of harness. Gated on calibrating the **load-band token thresholds** (the spine all fixtures hang from). Stored in the harness, not in this document, so the benchmark stays reproducible.

**Stopping rule.** Flat N, pre-committed (the scoping ruling below), same N across cells. The discipline it enforces: no observing a noisy cell and adding runs to tighten it — sequential peek-and-extend would quietly p-hack the variance downward.

### Experiment 1 — Stability run (tests H1)

Take the §8 seed task set. For each task, fix a load band and model and run a pre-committed flat **N (20–30) per cell via API** under the controls above, capturing the exact `usage` object (input, output, cache read/write, and any tool-result tokens). Compute per-cell variance on the dollar-weighted composite and per component.

- **Output:** which tasks band cleanly, which are intrinsically noisy (need a wider band or a split into sub-tasks).
- **Why API:** the API returns *exact* token counts per call — this is measurement, not estimation. The estimate only enters later, when extrapolating from measured archetypes to a customer's actual mix.

### Experiment 1c — Determinism A/B (tests H5, H6)

The causal follow-on to Experiment 1a's headline decomposition (output is ~100% of run-to-run cost variance ⇒ **Lever A: constrain the output**). 1a established the *mechanism* but could not test the *lever*: it has no skill-on/off contrast and stores no output to grade. 1c supplies both. It reuses the Experiment-1 harness, model ladder, and the frozen Phase-1a **input** artifacts; it adds calibrated-loose baseline prompts (`fixtures/manifest-phase1c.yaml`), a skill axis, and one new instrument (a frozen quality judge).

- **Design — a 2×3 factorial** on a few §8 tasks: **skill {off, on} × model {Haiku, Sonnet, Opus}**, flat **N=20**/cell (uniform with 1a). Tasks: the high-output-latitude generators **#6 (short-form copy)** and **#9 (long-form draft)**, where the lever should bite, plus **#4 (classify/triage)** as a deterministic **placebo control**, where it should not. Headline tier contrast: **Haiku vs Opus**. *Declared departure (see v0.6 log):* this varies two axes where 1a holds to one — justified because each pairwise contrast isolates one axis and the **skill×tier interaction is the H6 object**; the factorial is the right design for *causal* attribution, not the *observational* variance map of 1a.
- **The two arms (calibrated-loose baseline).** *Skill-off* is a **natural user request** — the structure lives in the skill, not the prompt — so a positive H5 measures *scaffolding*, not prompt verbosity (a fix from an independent review). *Skill-on* adds a frozen, hashed **output scaffold** (output schema/format + scope/length constraints) as a `system` block, **identical across all three models** (never tuned per model — invariant 6), applied in one pass (no hidden self-check; thinking is off in 1c). For #6/#9 the loose prompts **reuse the frozen Phase-1a input** blurb/brief; **#4 is mixed** — its baseline is the frozen Phase-1a constrained prompt (classification needs its label set) and its skill is a redundant procedure (a true placebo). Frozen + pinned by hash, replayable from the record (invariants 3, 5).
- **The quality judge (new, frozen, pre-registered).** Output text is now **persisted** (a posture change from 1a's tokens-only records, scoped to 1c). Deterministic graders where the task allows (#4 → exact-match; structured → schema-match). For the generative tasks, a **frozen LLM-judge**: fixed judge model, fixed rubric (2–4 quality checks/task — *directional intent; the FROZEN instrument is the source of truth and is slightly richer: #6 = 3 LLM checks incl. a 0–2 graded hook sensitivity-floor + 2 code-graded checks, #9 = 2 LLM + 2 code-graded checks; see `fixtures/judge/manifest.yaml` and `docs/phase1c-judge-spec.md` §2, frozen 2026-06-28 under `judge_hash` d12c36a2*), fixed prompt, thinking off, temperature omitted — run **K replicates with majority vote** so judge noise ≪ the measured effect. **Tier-equivalence via blind, order-randomized pairwise preference** (Haiku-output vs Opus-output: better / worse / tie), mitigating same-family self-preference bias, with a **human spot-check** on a sample to calibrate the judge. The judge config (model + rubric + prompt) is frozen and hashed like a fixture, pre-registered before any data — this is the operationalization the §5 measurement-target note requires for "Haiku does it identically."
- **Output:** does the skill (a) tighten output CoV / cut tokens (H5) and (b) close the Haiku→Opus quality gap (H6)? Cells where both hold are the strongest form of the over-service story — *a cheap model, scaffolded, matching an expensive one at lower and more predictable cost.* Cells where (b) fails are equally informative: they mark the work where tier still buys quality a skill can't supply.
- **Scope & honesty (lean design, pre-registered 2026-06-27):** **H5 is read on the output component** (`CoV_output` + mean output tokens), not the composite — the skill's fixed `system` block would otherwise lower composite CoV via **Lever B** and counterfeit an H5 win; total-token cost (incl. the skill's own input) is reported separately. The design is deliberately lean: **one frozen instance per task** (H6 shown per-instance), a **bundled** skill treatment (no mechanism ablation), and **no neutral-system control arm**. Caveats and the frozen judge contract live in `docs/phase1c-judge-spec.md`.

### Experiment 1d — Determinism A/B, de-confounded + widened (re-tests H5/H6; tests H7, H8)

The follow-on that fixes 1c's four self-declared limits (§5 1c block + §0 state-note 2026-06-28): no neutral-system control, a same-family judge, a format rubric that left H6's "narrows-the-gap" clause untestable, and one instance per task. 1d reuses the Experiment-1 harness and model ladder; it **extends** the pre-registration on **fresh tasks and a sounder instrument** — no 1c record is reinterpreted. **Pre-data** (no 1d measurement exists), so this extends rather than retrofits.

- **Design — a 3-arm × 3-model factorial** on ~9 §8 tasks: arms **{skill-off (calibrated-loose), neutral-system (length-matched, unstructured), skill-on (frozen scaffold)} × model {Haiku, Sonnet, Opus}**, flat **N=20** (uniform with 1a/1c). The neutral + skill arms apply to the ~8 generative tasks; **#4 stays the placebo** (its 1c treatment — no neutral arm, already ~0% CoV). ≈ 1,560–1,620 calls. *Declared departures (v0.7 log):* a **third arm** (still pairwise-isolable — each contrast moves one axis) and a **wider ladder** than 1c's three tasks.
- **The cap↔mandate ladder (H7).** ~8 generative tasks pre-classified **cap** vs **mandate** by `sign(scaffold length constraint − skill-off mean output)` — candidates *cap* #6/#7/#8, *mandate* #9/#13, plus 2–3 more to fill each side. Final identities + labels are **frozen in `runs/phase1d.yaml` before the run** (invariant 4); H7 tests whether the label predicts the sign of Δmean.
- **The neutral-system control (H8).** A frozen `system` block **per-task length-matched** to *that task's* real skill (within ~±10% tokens — not a blanket size), holding **generic, task-directed, structure-free** text: role/quality framing only (illustrative, frozen at build: *"You are a capable, professional assistant. Approach this task with care and use good judgment. Draw on relevant expertise and aim to be genuinely useful."*). It **excludes anything that changes *what* or *how much* the model writes** — no format/schema, no required sections, no scope list, and **no length guidance, including soft cues** ("concise / thorough / brief / detailed"). This isolates "the scaffold's *structure*" from "a task-directed `system` block is present and engaged" — the fair control (inert or off-topic filler would *under*-control, since a model ignores obvious junk). Identical text across models (invariant 6), frozen + hashed (invariants 3, 5).
- **The honest ruler (H6 re-test).** The 1c judge was Opus 4.8 — same family as one arm (self-preference, logged provisionally 2026-06-28). 1d swaps to a **cross-family** judge (a non-Claude frontier model; frozen, hashed, and **live-verified** before grading per "verify live") — an *instrument* change, not a model-under-test, so the §5 "Anthropic-first" product scope is untouched — with a **stratified human panel** validating it on a sample (the gold-standard check 1c deferred to its pending spot-check). Same blind, order-randomized, K-replicate-majority protocol.
- **Format-neutral rubric (makes H6 clause 2 testable).** Quality is graded on **substance independent of structure**, so skill-off/loose outputs become **eligible** for the blind pairwise — closing 1c's coverage hole (0 eligible skill-off pairs ⇒ H6's "narrows the gap *more than* a loose prompt" clause was unanswerable). 1d can finally evaluate **both** clauses of H6's kill-condition; **H6 and its ±10-pp margin are unchanged** — only the instrument improves.
- **Scope & honesty.** H5 still read on the **output component** (`CoV_output` + mean output), never the composite. H7 reads the sign of Δmean by cap/mandate group; H8 reads the neutral-vs-skill `CoV_output` ratio R (< ⅓ supported, ⅓–½ ambiguous, ≥ ½ killed); the H6 re-test reads the cross-family pairwise + the now-testable loose-vs-loose baseline. **Operational fixes:** the judge runs **under** the spend ceiling and **persists its token usage** (1c left it unguarded + unpersisted); the ceiling is bumped (≈ $100, confirmed by a pre-run micro-pilot estimate per the operational invariant). Fixtures, skills, neutral blocks, the cross-family judge harness, and `runs/phase1d.yaml` are a **separate build phase** (as 1c separated pre-registration from build).

### Benchmark harness (the automation layer for Experiments 1–2)

A repeatable runner that operationalizes the measurement:

- **Input:** task definitions (name, load-band fixtures, candidate models, iteration count).
- **Run:** each `(task × load band × model+tokenizer version)` combination N times via API, with thinking/effort settings as a tracked dimension. **Scoping ruled (2026-06-14):** (a) **Anthropic-first v1** — Haiku/Sonnet/Opus ladder only; OpenAI/Gemini deferred to the paid precision pass, since cross-provider token counts aren't comparable (different tokenizers) and the v1 provocation story is intra-Anthropic over-service, which the ladder alone tells. (b) **Flat N for v1**, not variance-weighted: at Anthropic-only scale the call budget that variance-weighting was meant to save mostly evaporates (~1–1.5k calls total), and uniform sampling is *cleaner for H1* — pre-allocating low N to "low-variance" cells would assume the archetype's variance, which is the thing H1 is supposed to test. Allocation-optimization is deferred until call budget actually binds. (c) Model dimension is **not fully crossed**: run each task at its hypothesized tier plus the over-service probe (one or more tiers down for the tier-pull tasks), not every model on every task. This, plus Anthropic-only, is what keeps the matrix in the low thousands.
- **Capture:** full usage breakdown per call — input, output, cache read, cache write, tool-result tokens — plus latency and wall-clock. **Stamp model+tokenizer version and run date on every record.**
- **Output:** per-cell **distributions** — mean, median, spread, **and tail** — not point estimates. Reporting the variance the field omits is itself the differentiator (see §10). This yields the benchmarked task→token library, which is the product's spine but **not** the durable moat: the static library commoditizes as the white space closes (§9). The moat is outcome-linkage and the living-meter discipline. Library stays deliberately small (~15–25 canonical tasks), expanded by *demand* (log uncovered tasks customers ask for — same flywheel as an escalation queue) and **re-run on each model release** to stay current.

> Build note: Claude Code for the harness; Gemini for prior-art deep research (see §9); Claude Design for the eventual front-end. The harness is the priority — it produces the data everything else depends on.

## 6. Analysis plan

- **H1:** coefficient of variation per task × load × model+tokenizer, across repeated identical runs, computed on the dollar-weighted composite (and per component to localize the spread — see §5 controls). Bandable if CoV < ~15–20%; otherwise flag for distribution-monitoring rather than point-estimate budgeting. Keep within-cell variance strictly separate from between-model variance — the latter is the over-service signal, not an H1 result.
- **H2:** bottom-up predicted vs. measured actual, per role; report whether within band.
- **H3 — design the analysis around the residual, not the confirmation.** Cache and fan-out obviously matter *somewhat*; testing whether they have *any* effect teaches nothing. Correct for both, then examine the residual for structure. **Pre-registered candidate additional factors (priority order): (a) tool/retrieval payload size** — few calls but a large file ingested each time, which neither cache (reuse) nor fan-out (call count) captures; **(b) multi-turn context accumulation** — conversation history resent every turn, flagged in prior art as possibly the largest single hidden cost; **(c) orchestration / retry overhead** — coordination and rework that eats projected margin. The seed set already carries payload-heavy entries (#7, #18, #19), multi-turn entries (#16, #17), and compound/orchestration entries (#20, #21); when fixtures are defined, ensure at least one clean probe isolates each of (a), (b), (c). If the residual loads onto any of them, the model gains a factor — that is the experiment working, not failing.
- **Price/dollar layer:** kept separate. Record raw token counts; apply current price scalars at report time only.

## 7. What we cannot conclude yet

Nothing about magnitudes. Earlier informal figures (e.g. "cache deflates ~3–5×," "fan-out ~5–50×") are **priors, not measurements** — placeholders to be replaced by Experiment 1–2 output. They are now pinned as falsifiable predictions in §3 (pre-registered before measurement) rather than left floating here. If the data disagrees, the data wins.

**Structural risk surfaced by the prior-art scan — staleness.** Tokenizer and price changes can obsolete a task→token figure within weeks (observed: Opus 4.7's tokenizer change ≈ a hidden price bump; the Claude Code per-developer day-rate roughly doubling over ~2 months). This is not a reason to abandon measurement — it is the reason the library must be a continuously re-run, version-stamped meter rather than a one-time benchmark. Treat any single run as current-as-of-a-date, never settled.

## 9. Prior-art scan — findings (resolved 2026-06-13)

The deep-research pass ran. Two independent reports came back; the more rigorous one was corroborated against primary sources, the other had confabulation tells (it "confirmed" a misattributed Willison claim, blurred between-model and within-task variance, and was stale on the Anthropic console). Verdicts by thread:

- **Measured task→token benchmarks — confirmed white space.** No public, reproducible, model-broken-down token *consumption distributions with run-to-run variance* exist for general knowledge work. Even GDPval (OpenAI's flagship knowledge-work benchmark) reports cost as averaged dollar *ratios*, not token distributions, and reports no variance. Coding-agent and academic-agent token economics are reasonably covered; general knowledge work is the gap. **This is the reason Experiment 1 exists — now verified, not assumed.**
- **Cost-driver taxonomy — solved.** Input/output asymmetry, caching, fan-out, multi-turn context accumulation, payload are all documented. Adopt, don't rebuild.
- **"FinOps for AI" — real, named discipline** (FinOps Foundation; framework + certification). Align vocabulary for buyer credibility.
- **Does visibility change behavior — yes, with caveats that reshape the product.** Showback decays (~10–20%/yr) and can backfire on framing; chargeback drives durable change. Live proof: Uber gave every employee a token dashboard and still blew its annual AI coding budget in four months. → Free tool = hook; paid accountability layer = the durable mechanism. (Feeds H4.)
- **Vendor-native governance — bare spend dashboards are redundant.** Anthropic now ships an Enterprise Analytics API with per-named-user cost attribution (early 2026); OpenAI, AWS, and Microsoft all expose per-user/team tracking. Do **not** build "make spend visible" as the product. *(Time-sensitive; current as of 2026-06-13.)*
- **Automatic model routing — real threat to model-selection advice; demoted accordingly.** Routing exists and is default-on at OpenAI (quality-contested), maturing fast. The "recommend the right model per task" feature is a bridge capability — do not anchor the product to it. Budgeting, waste-visibility, and outcome-linkage are more durable.

**Podcast leads, verified:** Willison's "~10% of salary" is a *misattribution* — he *analyzed* Uber's $1,500/tool cap as ~11% of median engineer comp; it was an observation, not a proposed budgeting rule. Autonomy × attribution is real (Simon-Kucher's "COMPASS"; Emergence Capital independently) — but it concerns *external product pricing*, adjacent to our internal-cost use case, and the Madhavan/Bloom attribution is unconfirmed. Coinbase routing-to-hold-costs-flat is confirmed (Armstrong, primary source).

**What remains genuinely open (where our own data adds value):** (1) task-level token *distributions* with variance for general knowledge work; (2) validated bottom-up role/team cost prediction (predicted-vs-actual — public validation is thin; FinOps states a 5%-of-actuals *goal*, not a demonstrated result); (3) which driver (output length / fan-out / multi-turn / payload) dominates the residual for *business* tasks.

**Caution.** Both reports share a prompt and a training zeitgeist, so their agreement is less independent than it looks. And the white space is **closing** — GDPval v2 is slated to add interactivity, Artificial Analysis already reports tokens, and data-analytics benchmarks now publish per-task token averages. The gap is real today but on a clock, which is *why* the moat is outcome-linkage and the living-meter, not the static task library.

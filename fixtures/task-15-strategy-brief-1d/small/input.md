[Context for a strategy brief]

Brightleaf Coffee is a regional chain of 18 cafés in the Midwest, known for locally roasted beans and a loyal in-store customer base. Revenue grew steadily until the last two years, when growth flattened. Store traffic is down about 8%, but average ticket size is up. A competitor recently launched a mobile app with order-ahead and a loyalty program and has been expanding aggressively. Brightleaf has no app; its loyalty program is a paper punch card. Leadership has some cash reserves but is cautious about large capital commitments. Younger customers increasingly ask about mobile ordering. The company has strong margins on its packaged retail beans, which it currently sells only in stores.

Leadership's question: how should Brightleaf respond to flattening growth and the competitive threat over the next 12 months?

[Incident Report — narrative]

On Tuesday around 2:15 PM, the customer-facing checkout service at ShopFlow began returning errors for a subset of users. Support started receiving complaints about failed payments around 2:20 PM. The on-call engineer, Alex Reyes, was paged at 2:28 PM. Initial investigation showed the payment-gateway integration was timing out. At 2:40 PM the team identified that a configuration change deployed earlier that afternoon had reduced the gateway connection-pool size, causing requests to queue and time out under normal load. The change was rolled back at 2:52 PM, and checkout success rates returned to normal by 3:05 PM. Roughly 1,400 checkout attempts failed during the window; affected customers were later sent an apology and a discount code. The team agreed to add an alert on gateway timeout rate, require a load review for connection-pool changes, and add the pool size to the deployment checklist. Severity was assessed as high (customer-facing revenue impact) but not critical (no data loss, contained within an hour).

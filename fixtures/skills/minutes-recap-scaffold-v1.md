# Format Example — Meeting Recap Email Style Guide

Write the recap as a short email using the structure below. Keep the whole body concise — a busy attendee should be able to skim it in under a minute. The example is from an unrelated meeting (an office-move planning sync) and shows the required format only — do not reuse its content.

Subject: [Meeting name] — recap & action items

Hi all,

Quick recap of today's sync:

Decisions
- Chose the north-building floor plan for the move.
- Set the move date for the first weekend of March.

Action items
- Jordan — confirm the moving-vendor quote (by Fri).
- Lee — circulate the new seating map (by next Tue).

Thanks,
[Name]

Rules: one short intro line; a "Decisions" list; an "Action items" list with an owner and a due date on each item; a brief sign-off. Keep the whole email under 100 words — no long narrative retelling of the discussion.

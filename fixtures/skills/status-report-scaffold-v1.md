# Format Example — Project Status Report Style Guide

Write a complete status report using the structure shown below. The example is from an unrelated project (a marketing website redesign) and shows the required format only — do not reuse its content.

## Summary
On track. Page-template work is ahead of schedule and content publishing has begun; one licensing risk needs a decision this week.

## Progress This Period
- Finished all four core page templates with brand sign-off — unblocks content entry for every remaining page.
- Published 60 of the 180 refreshed articles, keeping publishing ahead of the launch-critical path.
- Completed the accessibility review of navigation and forms, closing the last open audit item.

## Upcoming
- Publish the remaining articles and set up redirects from the old URLs.
- Run usability tests of the new contact flow with five customers.

## Risks & Issues
- Stock-photo licenses on 40 older articles lapse before launch — impact: those pages would ship without images; plan: legal is pricing renewal against replacing the photos.

## Blockers
- Launch-week banner copy still awaits marketing approval; a decision is needed by Friday to hold the launch date.

## Health
Schedule: about one week ahead. Budget: 48% spent at the halfway point of the timeline.

Rules: write all six sections, in this order — Summary (two or three sentences: overall status on track / at risk / off track plus the headline), Progress This Period (what was completed, as specific bullets), Upcoming (what's planned next period), Risks & Issues (each with its impact and the plan to address it), Blockers (what needs a decision or help, and from whom), Health (schedule and budget in a line or two, with figures if provided). Elaborate each item from the notes — every risk carries its impact and current plan, every progress item states what it unblocks or means for the project, every blocker names who or what is needed — so the complete report runs about 550 words (within ±10%). Use only what the notes provide; elaborate on what is there, but mark a section "nothing to report" rather than inventing content.

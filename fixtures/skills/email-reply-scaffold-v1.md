# Format Example — Customer Reply Style Guide

Write a short, professional reply using the structure below. Keep it brief and warm — acknowledge, address each concrete point, state the next step, and close. The example replies to an unrelated email (a customer who cannot update the email address on their account) and shows the format only — do not reuse its content.

Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out, and I'm sorry for the hassle.

- I've checked your account and found the old address was locked by a verification hold, which I've now cleared.
- I've sent a confirmation link to your new address — it's valid for 24 hours.

Once you click the link, the change takes effect immediately. If the link doesn't arrive within the hour, reply here and I'll sort it out directly.

Best,
[Name]

Rules: a one-line acknowledgment; a short bulleted response to each concrete issue the customer raised; one clear next step with a timeframe; a brief sign-off. Keep the whole reply under 100 words — no long apology paragraphs.

Meridian Regional Airways requires every flight to complete a standardized set of pre-flight checks before pushback. Today those checks are run from paper cards and laminated flip-books: completion is inconsistently recorded, and nothing systematically prevents an aircraft from beginning pushback with an incomplete checklist.

The Pre-Flight Checklist System (PFCS) shall present the flight crew with the correct checklist for the specific aircraft type and configuration, and require each item to be explicitly confirmed — or flagged as an exception with a reason. The system shall withhold a "ready for pushback" status until every mandatory item is confirmed, or a captain has authorized a documented override. It shall record which crew member completed each item and when, and retain a tamper-evident record of each flight's checklist for later review by operations and safety staff. PFCS shall integrate with the dispatch system so that a flight cannot be released for pushback unless PFCS reports the checklist complete. The system shall enforce role-based access for flight crew, dispatchers, maintenance, and safety reviewers, and shall alert a dispatcher whenever an override is authorized or a mandatory item is flagged. Because ramp and gate connectivity is intermittent, the system must remain fully usable offline and reconcile its records with the central store once connectivity is restored.
